Chapter 3: Multiple Integration

Double Integrals, Change of Order, Polar Coordinates & Applications

1. Introduction to Multiple Integration

Multiple integration is the extension of single-variable integration to functions of two or more variables. It is mainly used to calculate area, volume, mass, centroid and other physical quantities.

If a function depends on two variables, say f(x, y), then integration is performed over a region in the xy-plane.

∬R f(x, y) dA
Here, R represents a bounded region in the xy-plane.

2. Double Integrals

A double integral is an integral of a function of two variables over a region.

Iterated Integrals

Double integrals are evaluated as iterated integrals, integrating one variable at a time.

∫ab ∫g(x)h(x) f(x, y) dy dx

or

∫cd ∫p(y)q(y) f(x, y) dx dy
Inner integral is evaluated first, outer integral later.

3. Region of Integration

The region of integration defines the limits of the double integral.

Type I Region

Region bounded by x = a to b and y = g(x) to h(x).

Type II Region

Region bounded by y = c to d and x = p(y) to q(y).

Understanding the region is the key to solving double integrals correctly.

4. Change of Order of Integration

Sometimes changing the order of integration simplifies the problem.

We convert:

∫ ∫ f(x, y) dy dx → ∫ ∫ f(x, y) dx dy

Steps

  • Draw the region of integration
  • Identify new limits
  • Rewrite the integral
Very useful when inner integral becomes difficult.

5. Double Integrals in Polar Coordinates

For circular or symmetric regions, polar coordinates simplify calculations.

x = r cosθ , y = r sinθ
dA = r dr dθ

Polar Integral Form

∬ f(x, y) dA = ∫ ∫ f(r cosθ, r sinθ) r dr dθ
Always include extra factor r while converting to polar coordinates.

6. Applications: Area Using Double Integrals

The area of a region R can be found using:

Area = ∬R 1 dA

In polar coordinates:

Area = ∫ ∫ r dr dθ
Used to find area of circles, ellipses and bounded curves.

7. Applications: Volume Using Double Integrals

The volume under a surface z = f(x, y) over region R is:

V = ∬R f(x, y) dA

In polar coordinates:

V = ∫ ∫ f(r cosθ, r sinθ) r dr dθ
This formula is heavily used in engineering and physics.

8. Physical Interpretation

  • Double integral of density → Mass
  • Double integral of height → Volume
  • Double integral of 1 → Area
Multiple integration gives real-world meaning to abstract mathematics.
