1. Introduction to Optoelectronics

Optoelectronics is the branch of physics and electronics that deals with devices which emit, detect, or control light using semiconductor materials. These devices enable conversion between electrical energy and optical energy.

Optoelectronic devices form the backbone of modern technologies such as optical communication, lasers, LEDs, photodetectors, medical imaging, and sensing systems.

2. Nature of Light & Electromagnetic Spectrum

Light is a form of electromagnetic radiation visible to the human eye. It travels as waves and also shows particle nature in the form of photons.

4. LASER (Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation)

A LASER is a device that produces highly intense, monochromatic, coherent, and directional beam of light using stimulated emission.

Characteristics of Laser Light

  • Highly monochromatic
  • Highly coherent
  • Highly directional
  • High intensity

5. Population Inversion

Population inversion is a non-equilibrium condition where the number of atoms in the excited state (N‚ÇÇ) is greater than those in the ground state (N‚ÇÅ).

Population inversion is a necessary condition for laser action.

6. Components of Laser

  • Active Medium: Solid, liquid, gas or semiconductor
  • Pumping Mechanism: Electrical, optical or chemical
  • Optical Resonator: Two mirrors (one fully reflective, one partially)

7. Semiconductor Laser (Diode Laser)

A semiconductor laser is a p-n junction device that emits coherent light when forward biased. Photon generation occurs due to electron-hole recombination in the active region.

Advantages

  • Small size
  • Low power consumption
  • High efficiency

8. LED (Light Emitting Diode)

LED is an optoelectronic device that works on the principle of electroluminescence, where electrical energy is converted into light energy.

Advantages

  • Low power consumption
  • Long life
  • Fast switching

Applications

  • Display panels
  • Traffic signals
  • Digital devices

9. Photodiode

A photodiode is a light-sensitive semiconductor device that converts incident light into electrical current. It is operated in reverse bias.

Types

  • PN Junction Photodiode
  • PIN Photodiode
  • Avalanche Photodiode (APD)

10. PIN & Avalanche Photodiode

PIN Photodiode

Uses an intrinsic layer to increase depletion region width, improving speed and sensitivity.

Avalanche Photodiode (APD)

Operates at high reverse bias and provides internal gain through avalanche multiplication.
